MCat Wiki: Error Naming Conventions


To put it as simply as possible: errors thrown by MeowcatSoftware products are named with the the prefix "MCAT:ERR", then the major issue. The error ID (or error code, if you will) is a 2 digit hex number. The high bit is the major issue, the low bit is the minor issue. Example: if "MCAT:ERROR_NOT_IMPLEMENTED A0" is brought up, the error ID indicates that the requested feature is not implemented and no further information is available. Universal naming conventions are listed below:

  • NOT DOCUMENTED YET
  • NOT DOCUMENTED YET
  • NOT DOCUMENTED YET
  • NOT DOCUMENTED YET

The naming convention for errors and error codes is explained here. If you have further questions or encountered an undocumented error, head over to our Contact Us page and let us know right away.
